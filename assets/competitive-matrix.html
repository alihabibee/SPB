An Honest Comparison

Where Rest Wins
Capital risk. $1,000 demo vs. $80K+ device. Validated before you commit to inventory.
Operational simplicity. Zero chair time, 5-min training, no EHR integration. Doesn't compete for schedule slots.
Patient compliance. 84% daily adherence is unmatched. Patients use it because it feels good, not because they were told to.
Speed to margin. 4–12 week payback. Revenue from Day 1 of first patient sale.
Downside protection. Buyback guarantee. <2% returns. No consumable cost exposure.
Where In-Office Procedures Win
Per-visit revenue. $750–$1,500 per LipiFlow session vs. $500–$600 one-time Rest sale. Higher ticket per encounter.
Insurance billing. Some in-office procedures have emerging insurance coverage. Rest is cash-pay / HSA/FSA only.
Clinical immediacy. In-office thermal pulsation delivers measurable gland expression in a single visit. Rest works over time through daily use.
Established evidence base. LipiFlow has 10+ years of published clinical data. Rest's government-backed pilot is strong but the published literature is still building.
Perceived clinical authority. In-office procedures signal "serious treatment." Some patients want the doctor to do something, not prescribe something for home.

The Bottom Line

In-office procedures treat. Rest maintains. The question isn't which one — it's whether your patients have a home maintenance protocol that actually works. Right now, most don't. Warm compresses fail. Drops get forgotten. Rest is the first at-home option with verified daily adherence — and it costs your practice nothing to find out, because the demo-first path and buyback guarantee eliminate your risk.
