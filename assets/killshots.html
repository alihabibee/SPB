Killshot Options

Break-glass offers for when a qualified lead is hesitating after seeing Science, Workflow, AND Pricing.

Last Resort Only. These are high-value concessions. Only use when all other objections have been addressed and they're genuinely on the fence.
A

Reputation Insurance

For: Fear of being stuck with inventory
"Look, I understand the hesitation. Here's what I can do: if your patients don't love it, we'll buy back any unsold, sealed inventory within 90 days. Zero risk to you."

When it works: They're worried about inventory sitting on the shelf

B

Peer Validation

For: Need social proof
"Totally fair to be skeptical. Would it help to hear from another doctor in a similar practice? I can connect you with Dr. [Name] at [Clinic] — they were in your exact position six months ago."

When it works: They need to hear from a peer, not from you

C

The Time Lock

For: No urgency
"I'll be honest — we're allocating Spring 2026 inventory now because of 4-6 month lead times. I can't promise pricing or availability if we reconnect in a few months. But I also don't want to pressure you. What would help you make a decision this week?"

When it works: They're procrastinating without a real objection

Rules of Engagement

  • Never offer more than one killshot per conversation
  • Always re-qualify after: "Does that address your concern?"
  • If they still say no, respect it and move on gracefully
  • Log which killshot worked (or didn't) for future reference
