Activation
Days 31–60 · Marketing → Patient Stories → Optimization

Phase 2 focus: You've proven the workflow works. Now expand the funnel — more patients hearing about Rest, more reasons to say yes, and early patient outcomes to fuel the conversation.

Weeks 5–6 — Expand the Funnel
Goal: Every eligible patient exposed
Deploy patient education materials
Offer Cards in waiting room and checkout. Counter display near reception. Consider adding to intake paperwork or pre-visit email.
Staff
Finalize provider scripting
By now you know what language resonates. Lock in the recommendation script that feels natural. Role-play once with staff to align.
Doctor
Collect 2–3 patient stories
Ask early buyers: "How's it going with Rest?" Capture quotes (with permission) for recommending to the next patient. Peer stories convert better than clinical stats.
Staff
Activate in-office demo for undecided patients
For patients who hesitate at checkout, offer a 3-min demo. "Try it right now — if you like it, take one home." Track demo-to-purchase conversion separately.
Staff
Weeks 7–8 — Optimize + Track
Goal: Consistent 2+ units/week, objections resolved
Weekly staff huddle (10 min)
Quick standing meeting: units this week, top objection, one thing to try differently. Keep it short and action-oriented.
Team
Financial tracking vs. projection
Compare actual units sold against your base case. Are you at 1/week? 2/week? More? Calculate your actual margin to date.
Doctor
Follow up with early purchasers
Patients who bought in Weeks 2–3 have now used Rest for 4–6 weeks. Check in at their next visit. Positive experience → ask for referral or family member purchase.
Doctor
Evaluate pricing
Is your retail price working? If conversion is strong, you may have room to increase. If hesitation is price-driven, emphasize HSA/FSA harder before adjusting down.

Scale Decision
Days 61–90 · Performance Review → Volume Decision → Next Tier

Phase 3 focus: You have 60 days of real data. The question is no longer "does this work?" — it's "how much do we want to grow this?" This phase is about making a clear-eyed decision based on actual performance.

Weeks 9–10 — Performance Analysis
Goal: Clear picture of actual economics
60-day financial reconciliation
Total units sold, total revenue, total wholesale cost, actual margin. Compare to the business case projections. Where are you vs. conservative/base/high performer?
Doctor
Patient outcome review
Of patients who purchased in Month 1, how many reported improvement at follow-up? Any returns? Any complaints? This is your clinical confidence check.
Doctor
Staff workload assessment
Is recommending Rest creating friction or flow? Is anyone feeling burdened? Could workflow be streamlined further?
Team
Identify growth levers
What would move you from current volume to the next tier? More eligible patients identified? Higher conversion? Family/gift purchases? Extended recommendations beyond dry eye?
Doctor
Weeks 11–12 — Commit to a Path
Goal: Decision made, next order placed
Volume tier evaluation
At current run rate, does a larger order make sense? Moving from 12-unit to 24-unit orders saves $50/unit ($1,200/order). 48-unit saves $100/unit. Model the annual impact.
Doctor
90-day review call with Umay
Share your numbers, get benchmarked against the network, discuss volume pricing and growth strategies. This is a partnership conversation, not a sales call.
Doctor
Set Q2 targets
Based on 90-day actuals, set a realistic weekly target for the next quarter. Lock in order cadence and inventory levels.
Doctor
Decide: Scale, Maintain, or Reassess
Use the decision framework below. Be honest — the data tells the story.
