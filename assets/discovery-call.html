Discovery Call Script

8-12 minutes

You are not pitching. You are diagnosing.

Pre-Call Prep (30 seconds)

☐ Review clinic website
☐ Check booking form answers
☐ Did they open previous emails?
☐ Notes from prior touchpoints
1

Opener

30 seconds
"Dr. [Name], thanks for making time. I want to keep this focused — my goal is to understand your practice and see if there's a fit, not to pitch you. If it makes sense, I'll suggest a next step. If not, I'll tell you that too. Sound good?"

Wait for confirmation.
2

The Golden Question

60 seconds
"Let me start with the most important question: If you were going to add a new at-home therapy to your dry eye protocol, what's the one thing that would stop you?"

Shut up and listen.

They SayThey MeanYour Move
"Cost" / "Budget"Money concern→ Business Case + ROI Calculator
"Does it work?"Science concern→ Science/MOA One-Pager
"How does it fit?"Workflow concern→ Practice Fit Guide
"My staff won't..."Ops concern→ Practice Fit Guide
3

Validate Their World

2 minutes
"Can you walk me through what your current dry eye workflow looks like — from when the patient walks in to what you send them home with?"

Listen for:

  • What treatments do they offer?
  • What's their home care recommendation?
  • How do they handle compliance?
4

The Gap

1 minute
"It sounds like you're doing [X] really well. The challenge most clinics face is the gap between what they prescribe and what patients actually do at home. Is that something you see?"
5

The Pivot

30 seconds
If Money: "I can send you the math on what similar practices are seeing. Would that help?"

If Science: "I can send you the mechanism breakdown — we're the first non-pharmaceutical TRPM8 agonist. Worth a look?"

If Workflow: "I can show you exactly how this fits around your existing flow — zero chair time, no disruption to your optical conversion. Want me to send the fit guide?"
6

Close

30 seconds
"Based on what you've shared, I think [material] would be most helpful. I'll send that over today. When would be a good time to reconnect — maybe Thursday or Friday?"
