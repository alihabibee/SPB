Clinic Workflow SOP

The three-step process from patient arrival to device activation

1

Intake

Front Desk / Tech · 30 seconds

Trigger Question

During pre-exam intake, tech or front desk asks:

"Do your eyes ever feel tired, dry, or strained — especially after screen time?"

If Yes

Note on patient chart: "Candidate for Rest demo." No further action needed at this stage.

If No

Continue standard intake. No flag needed.

2

Doctor Consult

OD · 60 seconds

Review Flag

If patient was flagged at intake, acknowledge during exam:

"I see you mentioned eye fatigue. After your exam, I'm going to have my team show you something that helps with that — it's what I recommend to patients like you."

Prescription Language

If recommending Rest, use clinical framing:

"I'm prescribing a thermal therapy device for at-home use. It's clinically validated and most patients use it daily because it actually feels good. My team will set you up."

This positions Rest as a clinical recommendation, not a product upsell.

3

Handoff

Tech / Front Desk · 3-5 minutes

Demo (Optional but Recommended)

If time permits, offer a 3-minute in-office demo. Most conversions happen after the patient experiences it.

Checkout Script

"Dr. [Name] prescribed Umay Rest for your eye fatigue. It's [your clinic's price] and HSA/FSA eligible. Would you like to take one home today?"

QR Scan — Critical Step

Do not skip this. Have patient scan the QR code on the Start Here card before they leave. This links their device to their account and unlocks the guided sessions. Patients who skip setup have 3x lower adherence.

If They Decline

Hand them a Clinic Offer Card: "No problem. Here's a card with a special offer if you change your mind. The offer is good for 14 days."
