Part 2 — The 5D Dry Eye Integration Model

A Parallel Medical Pathway in 5 Steps

Rest isn't just a product you stock — it's the entry point to a dry eye service line. Five steps, each owned by the right person, each building on the last.

D1
Diagnose
Doctor · 20 seconds

Identify early gland dysfunction. Name it clinically. This is the moment that transforms Rest from a product into a treatment — and the recommendation from retail into medicine.

Doctor script
"I'm seeing early signs of gland dysfunction, likely contributing to your symptoms. Your tear system has two important parts — the lacrimal gland that produces tears and the meibomian glands that protect them with oil. Heat helps unblock oil glands, and controlled cooling can stimulate natural tear production and calm inflammation. We use both approaches depending on what we're seeing."
Then — the handoff
"We offer in-office treatments as well as a newer at-home therapeutic option called Rest that uses targeted temperature therapy. My team can show you a demo today…"
Branch by presentation
Symptomatic / workup warranted: "…and we'll book you back for a dry eye workup in a couple weeks."
Acute in-office (e.g., stye): "I'm going to have the staff set you up with a quick in-office treatment with Rest."
Mild / asymptomatic / not ready: Demo + offer card with QR code + book 2-week follow-up to reassess and discuss in-office treatments.

What this is not: No pricing. No long explanation. No added exam time. The doctor names the condition and hands off.

D2
Delegate
Staff · After frame selection or before checkout

The doctor's clinical authority does the heavy lifting. Staff don't "sell" — they fulfill a medical recommendation. Patients don't push back on "the doctor noted gland blockage" the way they push back on "would you like to add this?"

Staff script
"Dr. ___ noted early gland blockage. [show device] We can set you up with a quick 5-minute demo, or I can give you an info card to review at home."

D4

Two paths, not mutually exclusive. Rest is the entry point — not the end point.

Take-Home Start

Rest = Entry Point, Not Replacement. Patients who invest in early therapy are more likely to accept advanced in-office treatment because they understand the condition better.
D5
Develop
Doctor + Staff · 2-week follow-up

The follow-up closes the loop. This is where Rest stops being a product sale and becomes a clinical relationship — and where your dry eye service line grows.

2-Week Follow-Up Protocol
Evaluate
How often are they using it? Symptom improvement? Check compliance via app data if available.
→ Reinforces value of therapy
Reinforce
Share that 84% of patients use Rest daily. Normalize daily use as part of eye health maintenance.
→ Increases long-term adherence
Escalate
If symptoms persist or gland findings warrant it, introduce IPL / iLux / advanced testing. Rest prepared them to say yes.
→ Grows in-office procedure revenue

The insight that changes the conversation: Rest increases acceptance of advanced treatment. Patients who've used Rest for two weeks understand their condition, trust the process, and are primed for the next step. You're not selling IPL cold — you're recommending it to someone already invested in their eye health.
